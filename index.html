Zakynthos: The Greek Maldives

Crystal waters, hidden caves, and island life through my eyes.

“Zakynthos is known as the Greek Maldives for its turquoise waters, white beaches, and dramatic cliffs. It’s a place where nature feels alive, and every corner has a story.”

  • Blue Caves

Navagio (Shipwreck Beach)

Navagio is the image everyone knows — bright turquoise water, white cliffs, and the
famous shipwreck lying in the middle of the sand. You can only reach it by boat, and when
you arrive, the whole place feels unreal, like a painting. The water is so clear it almost
glows. Even if it’s crowded in summer, the view is unforgettable.

Blue Caves

The Blue Caves are a world of their own. Sunlight enters the caves and turns the water
deep blue, almost electric. Boats move slow there, so you can feel the cool air and see the
colors change on the rock walls. If you visit early in the morning, you’ll have the calm,
magical blue all to yourself.

Porto Limnionas

Porto Limnionas is a natural cove hidden between high rocks. The water is crystal clear and
deep, perfect for swimming and jumping from the cliffs. There’s no sand — just rock, sea,
and the smell of wild herbs. It’s one of the places where the island feels untouched, like
nature kept it for herself.

Turtle Spotting (Caretta-caretta)

Zakynthos is home to the famous Caretta-caretta sea turtles. You can often see them near
Laganas Bay, moving slowly under the water or coming up for air. The island protects them
carefully, because they nest on the beaches during summer. Watching a turtle swim next
to your boat is one of the most peaceful and special moments you can experience here.

“Zakynthos is more than a summer destination. It’s a rhythm, a feeling, a home for those who let the sea guide them.”
